1. INTRODUCTION
ITServiceFlow is an IT Service Management (ITSM) platform designed based on the ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) framework. The system helps organizations manage the entire lifecycle of IT services—from incident intake and request fulfillment to managing infrastructure changes—ensuring service continuity and quality.
2. BACKGROUND
IT departments often face chaos when receiving requests via fragmented channels like email or chat without standardized classification or prioritization. This leads to missed critical incidents, breached deadlines, and a lack of data for system improvement.
The Solution: A centralized ticket management system that applies standardized ITIL processes. This ensures transparency in accountability, measures KPIs (SLAs), and builds a Knowledge Base to resolve issues faster.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
•	Compliance: The system must comply with data security principles and maintain a detailed Audit Log for every change made.
•	Integration: Capability to integrate with Active Directory/LDAP for seamless employee data synchronization.
•	Availability: The platform must be highly available (99.9% uptime) as it is a critical business support tool.

5. GLOSSARY
•	Incident: An unplanned interruption to an IT service or reduction in the quality of an IT service.
•	SLA (Service Level Agreement): A documented agreement between a service provider and a customer that identifies both services required and the expected level of service.
•	CI (Configuration Item): Any component that needs to be managed in order to deliver an IT service (Hardware, Software, Documentation).
•	RFC (Request for Change): A formal proposal for an alteration to some product or system.
